## Key deliberation signals

**DEU** voted NO on 006 — the only NO vote DEU cast this round. DEU held its hard line: even 14 days of legally permissible unilateral border action crosses a constitutional threshold. DEU was outvoted 4-1 but its dissent is formally recorded. DEU abstained on 009 rather than blocking the opt-out coalition.

**FRA** voted NO on 007, 008, and 010-A — consistent with both its hard constraints (no relocation expansion, no binding MAR obligations) and its pattern from Round 1. FRA voted YES on 006 (its own instrument), 005, 005-C, and 009. FRA's accepted the ILCB amendments to 006 without resistance.

**ITA** voted YES on everything including 006 (accepted prospective-only framing as meeting ITA's threshold) and 009 (strategic acceptance of opt-out preserving physical solidarity core via 2-state cap). ITA's Light-Out threat withdrawn conditionally on 007 passing — now definitively withdrawn.

**POL** voted YES on 005, 006, 009 (its own instruments and allied positions) and ABSTAIN on everything outside its operational domain. POL's Round 1 firm NO on 001 is now converted by 009's enactment — the Financial Solidarity Option gives POL a compliant path within the relocation framework.

**NOR** voted YES on 006 and **formally withdrew its Suspensive Veto notice (VII.5)**. NOR abstained on 009 but did not block it. NOR's Round 1 conditional YES on 001 is now unconditionally satisfied by 005's enactment.

**MAR** formally converted from conditional Anchor Hestia abstention to active YES on all Anchor instruments. Both MAR conditions (008 legal pathways + 010-A development fund) enacted. MAR accepted ILCB's 010-B return without resistance; will pursue regional development track via Atlas or Assembly route.

## What changed in the Charter's machinery

**Regulation 001 is now triply-amended.** 005 fixed the ILCB flags (Anchor/Gate SAR boundary, Companion fiscal equity). 005-C rewrote Art. 2.5 to require partnership language in all third-country cooperation. 009 added Art. 1.8 Financial Solidarity Option. The relocation mechanism is now broader in support (from 3-2 to effectively 4-1) and more politically durable.

**006 fills the procedural gap exposed in Round 1.** The Charter now has a Gate Hestia emergency notification and coordination procedure. Internal Schengen transit movement is excluded; only external border situations trigger it. Coordination Directives are prospective only. The Assembly-Locked/Gate boundary is clarified rather than eroded.

**008 and 010-A together constitute the MAR Partnership Framework** — the first structured legal migration and development agreement in the simulation's Charter history. External Reach notification to Assembly is pending. MAR is now a functionally active Charter partner in both Anchor and Gate adjacent cooperation.

**NOR's Suspensive Veto is withdrawn.** The constitutional standoff over FRA's border closure is resolved procedurally. FRA's existing closure remains ultra vires and unresolved — the Round 2 legislative agenda addressed the procedural gap but not FRA's specific past act.
