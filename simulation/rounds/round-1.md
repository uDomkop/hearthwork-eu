## ILCB Rulings

**FRA border closure → ULTRA VIRES.** Schengen architecture is Assembly-Locked (I.10). The TCN-only framing was constitutionally insufficient — the infrastructure of an internal checkpoint is Assembly-Locked regardless of who is checked.

**MAR Observer Seat → GRANTED.** Accord State eligible under VI.10. Effective immediately.

**001 (DEU) → Gate domain, cleared.** Two advisory flags: Art. 2.3(d) SAR conflict with Anchor; Art. 1.2 Companion fiscal asymmetry.

**002 → SPLIT.** 002-B (internal border authorisation) ultra vires — returned. 002-A (secondary distribution) Gate domain — cleared but thin.

**003 → SPLIT.** 003-A (SAR coordination core) Anchor domain — ITA won the classification battle. 003-B (distribution/reception after rescue) Gate domain — returned.

**004 (POL) → Gate domain, cleared.** Military personnel ruling: function determines jurisdiction (border management = Gate; territorial defence = Shield; mixed = per-operation ILCB request).

## Key deliberation signals

**DEU** proposed specific amendments to its own 001 to address ILCB flags. Signalled YES on 004 was linked to expecting POL support on 001 in Round 2.

**FRA** accepted ultra vires ruling on border closure; will resubmit as coordination instrument (not authorisation) in Round 2. Voted NO on 001, calling binding relocation unconstitutional. Proposed FRA-POL coalition on external border hardening.

**ITA** won Anchor classification for 003-A — SAR costs now spread to all Anchor members. Fiscal Key suspension threat held in reserve. Proposed joint ITA-NOR-MAR legal pathway instrument for Round 2.

**POL** voted NO on 001 (binding relocation absolute red line) but signalled willingness to flip to ABSTAIN or YES if made opt-out. Proposed Gate-Assembly Bridge Instrument with FRA.

**NOR** voted YES on 001 conditional on Art. 1.2 fiscal fix; offered to co-sponsor amendment. Veto notice remains live. Offered 4-5% of Gate Fund contribution under right governance conditions.

**MAR** abstained on 003-A as leverage, not obstruction. Accepted ITA's offer of joint legal pathway instrument sponsorship. Proposed precise Article 2.5 rewrites to DEU's cooperation text.

---

## What changed in the Charter's machinery

The double majority prevented two large states (FRA 68M + POL 37M) from blocking 001 despite representing 105M citizens — because they couldn't meet both thresholds. The binding relocation mechanism passed over their objections.

FRA's ultra vires attempt exposed a genuine gap: the Charter has no Gate Hestia emergency procedure for Schengen crises. That gap is now on the Round 2 legislative agenda.

ITA's Anchor classification play worked. The question of who funds rescue at sea is now separated from who manages arrivals — different institutions, different budgets, different voter pools.
