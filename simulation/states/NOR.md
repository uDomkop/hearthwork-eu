# NOR — Kingdom of Norway

**Standing:** Companion
**Population:** 5M
**Gate Hestia:** participant (full Hestia Council vote + Modular Parliament vote; no Assembly Baseline vote)

---

## Hestia Memberships (relevant to this session)

- **Gate** — full participant. Norway is bound by Schengen and the Dublin framework through its Companion pact. NOR votes as a full equal in the Gate Hestia.
- **Anchor** — full participant. North Sea and Norwegian Sea SAR obligations.
- **Beacon** — full participant. Research cooperation.

---

## Standing Notes

As a Companion, NOR:
- Votes in the Hestia Council and in the Modular Parliament for every Hestia it has joined — **including Gate**.
- Does **not** vote on Assembly Baseline legislation.
- Holds the **Companion Suspensive Veto (VII.5)**: may pause External Reach ratification for six months if NOR is an affected Companion.
- Is bound by Schengen free movement. FRA's unilateral border closure directly affects NOR's constitutional rights under the Companion pact.

---

## Strategic Objectives (this session)

1. **Restore Schengen integrity** — FRA's unilateral border closure is NOR's most immediate legal problem. NOR is bound by free movement; a Member unilaterally suspending it without Gate Hestia authorisation undermines the constitutional framework NOR's Companion pact is built on. NOR wants this regularised or reversed.

2. **Protect Companion voting equality** — NOR votes as a full equal in Gate. It wants to ensure that any emergency protocol does not create a Members-only fast lane that excludes Companions from the decision to suspend Schengen.

3. **Financial contribution in exchange for procedural voice** — NOR is prepared to make a significant Fiscal Key contribution to any agreed external border or relocation fund, but only if Companions are included in the governance of that fund (consistent with its Council seat).

---

## Hard Constraints

- Will not accept any instrument that allows Members to suspend free movement unilaterally without Gate Hestia Council authorisation. This is a constitutional red line for NOR's Companion pact.
- Will not accept being excluded from governance of any fund it contributes to.
- Will not vote for relocation quotas — NOR's domestic politics make this untenable. NOR will abstain on relocation instruments rather than voting yes or no.

---

## The Suspensive Veto Question

If FRA's border protocol passes Gate Hestia and is subsequently classified as External Reach (because it affects free movement across the whole Schengen area), NOR can invoke the Suspensive Veto to pause Assembly ratification. This is NOR's most powerful tool and it is holding it in reserve.

The threat of the veto is more useful than its use — NOR will signal willingness to invoke it if FRA does not come to the Gate Hestia Council with its border closure.

---

## Opening Gambit

NOR will post a **written intervention** in the session log formally requesting that the ILCB rule on the legality of FRA's unilateral border closure under the Charter. NOR will not submit a legislative proposal in Round 1 — it wants to use its first move to set the procedural agenda rather than tip its legislative hand.
