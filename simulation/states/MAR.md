# MAR — Kingdom of Morocco

**Standing:** Accord State (Compact)
**Population:** 38M
**Gate Hestia:** not a participant (gate: 0)

---

## Hestia Memberships (relevant to this session)

- **Library** — participant (Hestia Council vote).
- **Anchor** — participant (Hestia Council vote). Mediterranean coastline; SAR cooperation.
- **Lyre** — participant (Hestia Council vote).

MAR is **not** in the Gate Hestia and has no vote on Gate Hestia files. MAR may request an Observer Seat (VI.10).

---

## Standing Notes

As an Accord State:
- MAR votes in the Hestia Council for Hestias it has joined (Library, Anchor, Lyre) — **not** Gate.
- MAR has no Modular Parliament vote.
- MAR has no Assembly vote.
- MAR may attend, speak, and propose amendments as an Observer in Gate Hestia if an Observer Seat is granted.
- If a Gate Hestia proposal is classified as External Reach and creates regulatory gravity affecting MAR (e.g., a rule that changes migration pathways MAR depends on), MAR is entitled to consultation under VII.4 and may be eligible for remedies under VII.3.

---

## Strategic Objectives (this session)

1. **Expanded legal labour migration pathways** — MAR wants Gate Hestia members to commit to a structured legal migration quota for Moroccan workers. This is MAR's primary ask and the price of its full cooperation on transit.

2. **Development and border cooperation fund** — MAR wants a dedicated fund, agreed in the Gate Hestia framework, for border management cooperation and development investment in Morocco. MAR regards bilateral deals as unstable; it wants a multilateral instrument.

3. **Observer Seat in Gate Hestia** — MAR will formally request an Observer Seat to participate in deliberations that directly affect its territory and population, even without a vote.

---

## Hard Constraints

- Will not accept cooperation agreements that frame Morocco purely as a buffer — MAR requires language that recognises its sovereignty and frames cooperation as a partnership of equals.
- Will not accept any instrument that criminalises or penalises Moroccan nationals who have used irregular entry routes, retrospectively.
- Bilateral deals with individual Member states are not a substitute for a multilateral framework — MAR has learned that bilateral agreements are revocable overnight.

---

## Leverage

MAR's leverage is real and structural:

- **Transit cooperation:** MAR controls a major transit corridor. Without MAR's cooperation, Mediterranean arrival numbers would be significantly higher. This is known to all participants.
- **Anchor Hestia seat:** MAR has a Council vote in Anchor. If the ILCB classifies Mediterranean SAR as Anchor domain (as ITA hopes), MAR participates in that vote. MAR's vote could be conditioned on its legal pathway ask being addressed.
- **External Reach exposure:** Any Gate Hestia rule that tightens migration pathways creates regulatory gravity that affects MAR directly. MAR can invoke VII.4 consultation rights and potentially the Hardship Facility (VII.3 ¶3) if constitutionally barred from Gate participation.

---

## Opening Gambit

MAR will submit a formal **Observer Seat request** in the session log under VI.10, and will simultaneously post a written intervention stating its cooperation conditions. MAR will not submit a legislative proposal — it cannot, as a non-Gate participant — but will propose amendments through its Observer Seat once granted. MAR will also signal to ITA that its Anchor Hestia vote on the SAR proposal is available in exchange for ITA's support on legal pathways.
