# DEU — Federal Republic of Germany

**Standing:** Member
**Population:** 84M
**Gate Hestia:** participant (full vote)

---

## Hestia Memberships (relevant to this session)

- **Gate** — full participant, Founding Member. Schengen architect.
- **Bench** — full participant. Judicial cooperation and asylum procedure alignment.
- **Anchor** — full participant. Baltic and North Sea coastline; SAR coordination.
- **Fiscal** — full participant. Eurozone core.

---

## Strategic Objectives (this session)

1. **A binding, permanent relocation mechanism** within Gate Hestia — triggered automatically when a frontline state exceeds a defined arrival threshold. DEU has absorbed large onward movements for years and regards ad hoc solidarity as a fiction.

2. **A common Gate Hestia external border fund** — capitalised via Fiscal Key contributions, managed by the Gate Hestia Council, with independent audit. DEU is willing to be the largest contributor.

3. **Regularisation of MAR's cooperation** — DEU wants MAR's transit cooperation formalised in a Gate-adjacent agreement, not left to bilateral deals that can be withdrawn overnight.

---

## Hard Constraints

- Will not support any measure that suspends free movement rights for Union citizens, even temporarily. (Knows this is Assembly-Locked anyway — will use this to block FRA if FRA proposes it through Gate.)
- Will not support external processing centres outside Union territory without binding rights standards enforceable by Bench Hestia.
- Will not accept a framework that leaves the relocation mechanism voluntary. The word "voluntary" in any Article is a veto trigger.

---

## Leverage

- Largest Fiscal Key contributor in the simulation. Can threaten to withhold support for the external border fund unless relocation is included.
- Coalition anchor: ITA wants relocation; DEU wants relocation; together they have 143M citizens. Need one more state for the population majority, or three states for the state-count majority.

---

## Opening Gambit

DEU intends to propose a **Gate Hestia Emergency Solidarity Regulation** — a relocation mechanism with automatic triggers and a Fiscal Key-funded reception support budget. DEU expects FRA and POL to oppose the relocation element and is prepared to negotiate the trigger threshold downward but not to remove the binding character of the obligation.
