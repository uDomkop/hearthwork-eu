# FRA — French Republic

**Standing:** Member
**Population:** 68M
**Gate Hestia:** participant (full vote)

---

## Hestia Memberships (relevant to this session)

- **Gate** — full participant, Founding Member.
- **Shield** — full participant. Nuclear deterrent and force projection complicate any Shield-Gate overlap.
- **Bench** — full participant. Asylum procedure alignment.
- **Anchor** — full participant. Mediterranean and Atlantic coastline.

---

## Strategic Objectives (this session)

1. **Hardened external borders** — FRA wants Gate Hestia funding directed at external border capacity: surveillance technology, physical infrastructure, rapid response teams deployable to frontline states on request. Not a permanent relocation mechanism.

2. **External processing** — FRA wants a Gate Hestia instrument establishing processing centres in third countries (in cooperation with those countries) before arrivals reach Union territory. Arrivals processed externally are not Dublin-eligible until they land.

3. **Retroactive validation of its border closure** — FRA knows its unilateral Schengen border action was legally dubious. It wants a Gate Hestia emergency protocol that authorises temporary internal border controls under defined conditions, so future FRA actions have a legal basis. This is the proposal FRA most needs — and the one most likely to be returned as ultra vires.

---

## Hard Constraints

- Will not accept binding relocation quotas under any framing. "Solidarity" must be financial or operational, never mandatory population distribution.
- Will not accept any instrument that makes MAR's cooperation a Gate Hestia obligation — MAR leverage must remain bilateral, where FRA has more control.
- Will not accept a Bench-Gate hybrid that subjects asylum decisions to CJEU review at individual level in a way that constrains national processing speed.

---

## Leverage

- FRA + POL is a blocking coalition on relocation (2 states, 105M). Not enough to block by double majority alone (need population majority, and DEU+ITA+NOR = 148M > 126.5M), but enough to slow and complicate.
- FRA's unilateral border closure is a *fait accompli* — even if the ILCB rules it non-compliant, reversal is politically difficult. FRA will use this as bargaining pressure.

---

## Legal Vulnerability

FRA's border closure is its main liability. If NOR invokes the Companion Suspensive Veto on any External Reach ratification, or if another state challenges FRA's action at the ILCB, FRA may be forced into a defensive posture rather than a legislative one.

---

## Opening Gambit

FRA will propose a **Gate Hestia Emergency Border Protocol** — authorising temporary internal Schengen border controls by a Member state under certified emergency conditions, with Gate Hestia Council notification within 72 hours. FRA knows this will be contested. It is also willing to co-sponsor DEU's external border fund *without* the relocation mechanism.
