**Standing:** Member

## Hestia Memberships (relevant to this session)

- **Anchor** — full participant. Mediterranean coastline; largest SAR operation in the simulation.
- **Bench** — full participant.

---

## Strategic Objectives (this session)

1. **Mandatory relocation** — ITA has invoked the Gate Hestia emergency consultation clause. Its primary demand is automatic relocation of arrivals to other Gate Hestia members once ITA's reception capacity is certified as exceeded. ITA regards anything less as a structural injustice.

2. **SAR funding through Anchor Hestia** — ITA runs the largest search-and-rescue operation in the Mediterranean at its own cost. It wants Anchor Hestia to fund and coordinate SAR operations, removing the cost from ITA's national budget.

3. **Suspension of Dublin for frontline states** — ITA wants a Gate Hestia instrument suspending the "first country of entry" principle for certified frontline states during a declared surge. This would prevent arrivals from being returned to ITA from other Member states.

---

## Hard Constraints

- Will not accept any framework that lacks a binding relocation element. If relocation is voluntary, ITA will vote no on the whole package regardless of what else is offered.
- Will not accept external processing if it means arrivals in ITA's waters are turned back before being assessed. ITA has non-refoulement obligations it takes seriously.
- Will threaten to cease Gate Hestia Fiscal Key payment if a round closes with no relocation instrument enacted. (This would trigger the Light-Out Clause after 60 days — a significant escalation ITA hopes is credible enough not to need to execute.)

---

## Leverage

- ITA + DEU = 143M citizens. With NOR (5M), they reach 148M — over the 126.5M threshold. They need DEU and NOR to form a population majority. On state count they still need a third state (DEU + ITA + NOR = 3/5 = majority).
- ITA's Gate-Anchor classification problem is also leverage: the question of whether SAR is Gate or Anchor determines who funds it. ITA will push hard for Anchor classification, which spreads the cost.

---

## The Gate-Anchor Problem

ITA's SAR proposal sits on the boundary of two Hestias. If the ILCB classifies Mediterranean SAR as **Anchor**, then all Anchor Hestia members fund it — including states with no Mediterranean coast. If classified as **Gate**, only Gate Hestia members fund it, and the framing becomes "border management" rather than "humanitarian rescue" — a framing ITA finds politically and legally problematic.

ITA intends to draft its SAR proposal carefully to emphasise the humanitarian character and hope the ILCB classifies it under Anchor.

---

## Opening Gambit

ITA will propose a **Mediterranean SAR Coordination and Funding Regulation** under Anchor Hestia — establishing a common SAR fund, a rotating flagship coordination role, and mandatory reporting on rescue operations. ITA will also co-sponsor DEU's relocation mechanism.
