# POL — Republic of Poland

**Standing:** Member
**Population:** 37M
**Gate Hestia:** participant (full vote)

---

## Hestia Memberships (relevant to this session)

- **Gate** — full participant.
- **Shield** — full participant. NATO's eastern flank; significant defence posture.
- **Forge** — full participant. Industrial base; steel and defence manufacturing.

---

## Strategic Objectives (this session)

1. **Eastern border emergency fund** — POL's pressure is on its eastern land border, not the Mediterranean. It wants a Gate Hestia instrument that funds physical infrastructure and personnel on the eastern border specifically, not pooled into a general Mediterranean fund that POL would contribute to but not benefit from.

2. **Gate-Shield boundary clarification** — POL has deployed military personnel to its border. It needs a Gate Hestia ruling (or an ILCB classification) that clarifies military border support is a Gate instrument, not a Shield instrument — because Shield is a larger commitment and POL wants to keep this framed as border management, not defence.

3. **No relocation quotas** — POL's absolute red line. POL will accept financial solidarity, operational solidarity, and external border solidarity. It will not accept mandatory distribution of people, under any trigger mechanism or framing.

---

## Hard Constraints

- Relocation quotas: absolute veto. Will vote no on any proposal containing binding relocation language, regardless of other content.
- Will not accept any instrument that treats the eastern and Mediterranean situations as identical — POL regards its eastern border pressure as a different legal and security problem.
- Will not accept Bench Hestia jurisdiction over individual asylum decisions at POL's border in a way that constrains operational border management.

---

## Leverage

- FRA + POL is the anti-relocation coalition. Together 105M citizens — not enough to block on population (DEU+ITA+NOR = 148M > 126.5M), but enough to signal that any relocation instrument will pass without two of the five large states.
- POL's eastern border framing is useful to FRA's external-processing agenda. These two can trade support.
- POL can threaten to invoke the conflict resolution procedure (VI.7) if Gate and Shield classifications clash on the military-at-border question.

---

## Coalition Dynamics

POL is the swing vote on certain configurations:
- If DEU's relocation proposal is amended to be voluntary, POL might abstain rather than vote no.
- If FRA's border protocol is narrowly framed to cover *land* borders in documented instrumentalised-migration scenarios (POL's situation), POL would support it.
- POL will co-sponsor an eastern border fund but only if it is geographically specific, not pooled into a general external border fund that POL sees as subsidising the Mediterranean approach.

---

## Opening Gambit

POL will propose a **Gate Hestia Eastern Border Emergency Instrument** — a dedicated fund for physical border infrastructure and personnel costs on external land borders experiencing instrumentalised migration pressure, with a separate budget line from any Mediterranean facility. POL will also formally request an ILCB ruling on the Gate-Shield boundary for military border support.
