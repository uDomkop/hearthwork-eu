# Amendment to Gate Hestia Emergency Solidarity and Border Management Regulation (001)

## Preamble

The Gate Hestia Council enacted Regulation 001 on a narrow coalition basis (3-2, 148M-105M). The ILCB has issued two advisory flags: one concerning Article 2.3(d)'s reference to Search and Rescue operations, which creates potential conflict with Anchor Hestia's enacted SAR coordination instrument (003-A); and one concerning Article 1.2's asymmetry in imposing relocation obligations on Companions without a corresponding fiscal floor.

DEU proposes targeted amendments to both Articles to:

1. **Clarify Article 2.3(d)** to make explicit that Gate Hestia does not directly fund SAR operations. Gate may fund coordination logistics with Anchor Hestia's rescue mechanisms but does not replicate or substitute Anchor's Common Maritime Rescue Fund. This eliminates the ILCB's conflict flag.

2. **Establish a binding Companion Fiscal Key minimum** of 2.5% of the annual Fund budget, ensuring that Companions who participate in relocation governance bear meaningful financial responsibility. This addresses the fairness flag and was signalled in Round 1 by NOR, who co-sponsors this amendment.

These amendments do not disturb the binding character of Article 1's relocation mechanism, nor do they alter the Fund's foundational capitalisation.

---

## Amendment 1: Article 2.3(d) — Clarification on Search and Rescue

### Current Text (Article 2.3(d))
```
(d) Search and rescue operations in international waters within Gate jurisdiction;
```

### Amended Text
```
(d) Coordination of search and rescue disembarkation logistics with Anchor Hestia's Common Maritime Rescue Fund mechanisms (including Single Maritime Authority deployment and port preparation for rescued persons), but not direct funding of SAR operations at sea, which remain the responsibility of Anchor Hestia instruments enacted under Article VI.2;
```

### Rationale

The enacted Regulation 003-A (ITA) established Anchor Hestia's maritime rescue coordination and funding framework, with costs spread across all Anchor members. Article 2.3(d) of Regulation 001 inadvertently created ambiguity by referring to "Search and Rescue operations" as a Gate Hestia eligible expenditure, raising questions about whether Gate would duplicate or conflict with Anchor's cost-sharing model.

This amendment clarifies the division of labour:

- **Anchor Hestia (003-A)** funds the rescue operation itself (vessels, crews, coordination at sea, Single Maritime Authority).
- **Gate Hestia (001, as amended)** funds the logistics of *receiving* rescued persons after disembarkation (port preparation, transfer to receiving states under Article 1's relocation mechanism, reception capacity in destination states).

This is operationally coherent and eliminates the ILCB conflict flag. DEU remains committed to robust maritime rescue coordination and will allocate sufficient Gate Fund resources to reception and disembarkation logistics to support Anchor's rescue operations.

## Amendment 2: Article 1.2 — Companion Fiscal Key Contribution Minimum

### Current Text (Article 1.2)
```
### 1.2 Mandatory Relocation Obligations

Upon the Council's declaration under 1.1, all Member States and Companions in the Gate Hestia are bound by the following relocation obligation:

Each state shall accept and assume full responsibility for persons designated for relocation by the burdened frontline state, calculated as follows:

**Relocation share = (State population ÷ Total Gate population) × (Persons exceeding 1,500/mm threshold)**

No state may declare this obligation "voluntary" or decline it without material consequence to its standing in the Gate Hestia Council and its Fiscal Key allocation (see Article 2).
```

### Amended Text
```
### 1.2 Mandatory Relocation Obligations

Upon the Council's declaration under 1.1, all Member States and Companions in the Gate Hestia are bound by the following relocation obligation:

Each state shall accept and assume full responsibility for persons designated for relocation by the burdened frontline state, calculated as follows:

**Relocation share = (State population ÷ Total Gate population) × (Persons exceeding 1,500/mm threshold)**

No state may declare this obligation "voluntary" or decline it without material consequence to its standing in the Gate Hestia Council and its Fiscal Key allocation (see Article 2).

**Companion Fiscal Minimum:** Any Companion state participating in the Gate Hestia shall contribute financially to the External Border and Reception Fund (Article 2) at a minimum rate of **2.5% of the annual Fund budget**, calculated on the basis of proportional relocation obligations. A Companion may elect to exceed this minimum contribution or may substitute in-kind provision of reception capacity; the Council shall determine equivalence. Failure to meet the 2.5% minimum shall trigger the same consequences as non-performance of relocation obligations under the final sentence above.
```

### Rationale

The enacted Regulation 001 imposes mandatory relocation obligations on Companions (e.g., NOR) on an equal basis with Member States, weighted by population. However, the current Article 2.2 permits Companions to "opt in" to financial contributions rather than to pay mandatory Fiscal Key shares.

This creates an asymmetry: Companions exercise voting rights in the Gate Hestia Council (Article 2.4 references "all Member States and Companions"), participate in relocation governance, and receive relocation costs reimbursed from the Fund, but do not bear proportional financial obligation to capitalise it.

NOR has signalled willingness to contribute meaningfully to a fund it helps govern. This amendment establishes a binding floor that ensures Companion participation in funding is real, not optional. The 2.5% figure is calibrated to reflect NOR's proportional relocation burden (approximately 0.5M of 253M total Gate population; 0.5% population share × relocation trigger volume yields a Fund contribution of approximately 2-3% of total for NOR under normal burden distribution).

Companions may exceed this minimum or substitute in-kind reception capacity; the amendment preserves flexibility in *how* the obligation is met, while ensuring the *binding character* of the obligation itself.

## Impact on Existing Articles

These amendments do not require changes to any other Articles. They are surgical fixes to the two ILCB advisory flags and do not disturb:

- The automatic relocation trigger and thresholds (Article 1.1);
- The relocation rights and procedures (Articles 1.3–1.7);
- The Fund's core capitalisation and governance (Article 2.1–2.2, 2.4);
- The third-country cooperation framework (Article 2.5, unaffected by SAR clarification);
- Relationship to Schengen and Bench Hestia (Articles 3–4);
- Entry into force (Article 5).

---

## Voting Implications

DEU notes that this amendment may garner broader support than the original Regulation 001, particularly from Companions concerned about proportional governance participation and from states seeking clarification on Anchor–Gate institutional boundaries.

DEU does not require any state to vote on the amendment as a package; amendments may be voted separately if the Council prefers.

---

## Fiscal Implications

The 2.5% Companion Fiscal Minimum does not increase the Fund's total capitalisation. It redistributes the burden among participating states: instead of Member States covering the full 100% of the annual budget, Companions now contribute proportionally to their relocation obligations (minimum 2.5% of Fund total). This is a neutral or slightly beneficial adjustment for large contributors like DEU, as it spreads costs more equitably.

No upward revision of the Fund budget is required.
