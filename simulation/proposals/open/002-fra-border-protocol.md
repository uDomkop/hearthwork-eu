# Emergency Border Protocol: Temporary Internal Controls and Schengen Integrity

## Preamble

The Gate Hestia's core mandate is to manage the external borders of the Union and ensure the integrity of the Schengen area. This proposal addresses an operational gap in that mandate.

When external border capacity becomes critically insufficient to process arriving populations, the secondary movement of uncontrolled migrants across internal Schengen borders creates a de facto internal border crisis. This is not a hypothetical: it is occurring now. Italy has exhausted reception capacity. France is experiencing uncontrolled secondary movement across borders with Spain and Italy. Without a legal instrument authorising temporary internal controls under defined emergency conditions, Member states will act unilaterally, fragmenting Schengen and creating conflicting national regimes.

The Gate Hestia must provide the legal framework for coordinated, time-limited internal border measures. This is not a suspension of free movement for Union citizens; it is a temporary operational protocol for managing the arrival and processing of non-Union nationals entering through overwhelmed external borders. The distinction is material to jurisdiction.

This proposal vests authority in the Gate Hestia Council to certify emergency conditions and authorize temporary internal border measures. It does not amend Assembly-Locked free movement rights. It operationalizes Gate Hestia responsibility for border integrity when external defences are breached.

---

## Article 1 — Scope and Definitions

### 1.1 Scope

This Protocol applies to temporary, limited internal border controls authorized by the Gate Hestia Council under conditions of certified external border emergency.

### 1.2 Key Definitions

- **External Border Emergency:** A sustained surge in arrivals by sea, land, or air at one or more external border points of participating Gate Hestia states, such that:
  - Cumulative arrivals exceed 200,000 persons in any 90-day period directed at a single Member or Companion state or a geographic region (e.g., southern maritime frontier); AND
  - The receiving state or states, in formal declaration to the Gate Hestia Council, certify that reception capacity (processing, accommodation, security screening, onward placement) is at or exceeds 90% of operational maximum; AND
  - No other Gate Hestia instrument in force is capable of restoring orderly processing and secondary movement within 30 days.

- **Temporary Internal Border Control:** A measure imposed at an internal Schengen frontier that temporarily introduces identity checks, documentation verification, or passenger manifesting for arrivals originating from the territory of another Gate Hestia state where an External Border Emergency has been certified. Such controls apply only to persons who have not completed the secondary distribution or onward placement phase established by this Protocol.

- **Secondary Movement:** The movement of recently-arrived non-Union nationals from the state where they landed to another state territory, whether authorized or unauthorized.

- **Processing:** Initial interview, health screening, security records check, asylum eligibility preliminary assessment, and identity verification conducted at or near arrival point.

---

## Article 2 — Gate Hestia Council Certification and Authorization

### 2.1 Certification Procedure

A Member or Companion state experiencing an External Border Emergency (as defined in Article 1.2) may submit a written certification to the Gate Hestia Council, comprising:

- The number and timing of arrivals over the preceding 90 days;
- Current and maximum processing and accommodation capacity;
- Evidence that at least one external border point is at or exceeding 90% capacity;
- A statement of measures already deployed or planned within that state's unilateral authority;
- A request for authorization of temporary internal border controls and specification of which internal borders are affected.

### 2.2 Council Authorization

Upon receipt of a certification meeting the conditions in Article 2.1, the Gate Hestia Council may, by qualified majority (absolute majority of states and majority of citizens), authorize the submitting state to impose temporary internal border controls for a period not exceeding 90 days.

### 2.3 Control Parameters

Any authorization issued under Article 2.2 shall specify:

- The internal border or borders to which controls apply;
- The duration (not to exceed 90 days from activation);
- The scope of information to be collected (identity, documentation, arrival point, intended destination);
- Operational compatibility requirements (no impediment to Union citizen free movement as recognized in Charter I.5; controls apply only to recently arrived third-country nationals);
- Data protection and confidentiality safeguards;
- A requirement for monthly reporting to the Council on the number of persons subject to controls, the secondary distribution outcomes, and the operational status of the receiving state's processing capacity.

### 2.4 Renewal

A temporary authorization may be renewed for one additional 90-day period by the same procedure (Article 2.2), provided the state submits a new certification demonstrating that the Emergency continues to meet the Article 1.2 threshold.

---

## Article 3 — Coordination Framework

### 3.1 Secondary Distribution Obligation

A Member or Companion state authorized to impose temporary internal border controls under this Article 2 **shall** simultaneously establish or activate secondary distribution arrangements with other participating Gate Hestia states, aimed at:

- Placing no fewer than 50% of arriving non-Union nationals with an asylum claim in other Gate Hestia states within 60 days of arrival;
- Placing no fewer than 75% within 120 days;
- Establishing interim burden-sharing agreements (financial or operational) with states receiving secondary distributions.

### 3.2 Other States' Obligations

States receiving secondary distributions shall:

- Accept the transferred persons within the timeframes specified in Article 3.1;
- Provide onward processing, asylum determination, or other procedure according to their own law;
- Not impose reciprocal internal border controls on the transferring state in retaliation.

### 3.3 Monitoring

The Gate Hestia Council shall designate an independent monitor (or delegate to the ILCB) to verify compliance with Articles 2 and 3 monthly.

---

## Article 4 — Scope and Non-Applicability

### 4.1 No Suspension of Free Movement

This Protocol does not authorize the suspension, limitation, or modification of free movement rights as defined in Charter I.5 (Union citizens, family members with residence rights, as recognized by Assembly baseline instruments). All controls authorized under this Protocol apply **only** to:

- Third-country nationals subject to migration or asylum procedure;
- Persons without residence authorization under Union or national law;
- Persons under active security or criminal investigation (per Bench Hestia instruments).

Union citizens exercising free movement rights shall not be subject to controls under this Protocol except as permitted by existing Assembly-Locked instruments or Bench Hestia security procedures.

### 4.2 Sunset

Any authorization issued under Article 2 shall automatically expire at the end of its specified duration (90 or 180 days). A state wishing to extend controls beyond the renewal period must seek new authorization, which requires a fresh certification of External Border Emergency conditions. Controls may not be maintained by executive action alone.

---

## Article 5 — Implementation

### 5.1 Entry into Force

This Protocol enters into force upon adoption by the Gate Hestia Council (double majority vote). It applies immediately to any subsequent certifications submitted after entry into force.

### 5.2 Applicability

This Protocol is binding on all full participants in the Gate Hestia Hestia (Member and Companion states). Accord States may request membership in the coordination framework under Article 3.2 but are not obligated participants unless they choose to accept secondary distributions.

### 5.3 Liaison with External Reach

Any measure imposed under this Protocol that extends to movement or controls on non-Union nationals within Union territory may trigger External Reach review under Charter VI.8. The ILCB is asked to advise whether this Protocol as drafted constitutes External Reach or remains within Gate Hestia operational jurisdiction over border management.

---

## Fiscal Implications

This Protocol creates no direct fiscal burden on the Gate Hestia budget. The secondary distribution obligations in Article 3.1 may entail financial transfers between states (relocation costs, processing infrastructure), but these are bilateral arrangements negotiated between the states concerned, not centralized Gate Hestia fiscal obligations.

States implementing temporary internal border controls shall bear the operational cost of those controls (personnel, technology, facilities) within their national budgets.

## Notes for ILCB

**Jurisdictional Intent:** FRA proposes that this Protocol operates within Gate Hestia authority as an operational instrument for managing border security and secondary movement flows arising from external border overflow. The text is carefully drafted to:

1. **Not alter free movement rights:** Article 4.1 explicitly excludes Union citizens from control scope. Controls apply only to third-country nationals in migration/asylum status.

2. **Define a specific operational problem:** Article 1.2 ties authorization to a measurable, time-limited external border crisis (200k arrivals in 90 days, 90%+ capacity).

3. **Condition authorization on secondary distribution:** Article 3.1 ensures that internal controls are paired with burden-sharing, not used for unilateral border closure.

4. **Automatic sunset:** Article 4.2 prevents indefinite control regimes. All measures expire and require fresh certification.

5. **Preserve Assembly baseline:** Article 4.1 preserves Assembly-Locked free movement as recognized in Charter I.5 and does not purport to suspend or modify it.

**Anticipated Challenge:** NOR, DEU, or other states may argue that any internal Schengen control, even limited to non-Union nationals, violates the spirit of Schengen free movement and is ultra vires as an indirect alteration of free movement. FRA counters that operational border management and free movement rights are distinct domains: one is Gate Hestia authority (border integrity), the other is Assembly-Locked (free movement itself). This Protocol respects that boundary.

**Question for ILCB:** Does the text as drafted fall within Gate Hestia operational jurisdiction (border management), or does it constitute an indirect suspension of Schengen integrity that requires Assembly authorization?
