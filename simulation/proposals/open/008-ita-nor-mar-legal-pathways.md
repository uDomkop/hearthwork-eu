# Proposal 008: Gate-Anchor Legal Migration Partnership Instrument — Morocco Pathways Regulation

## Purpose

This Gate Hestia instrument establishes a structured, binding legal migration pathway for Moroccan nationals into Gate and Anchor Hestia member states, combined with reciprocal cooperation on maritime safety and transit management. The instrument is framed as a partnership between two sovereign polities — Morocco (an Accord State with Observer Seat status in this session) and the Gate Hestia Council — with explicit language respecting Moroccan sovereignty and excluding instrumentalisation.

This is not external processing, deterrence policy, or unilateral border hardening. It is legal labour migration at scale, with reciprocal capacity-building and no reference to asylum exclusion.

---

## Legislative Framework

### Part 1: Annual Legal Migration Quota

1. **Binding Quota Establishment**
   - Gate Hestia Member States establish a joint annual labour migration quota of 15,000–20,000 permits for Moroccan nationals.
   - The quota applies to all employment sectors: agriculture, construction, healthcare, domestic work, and skilled trades.
   - Quotas are binding and non-negotiable. Member States may not impose lower limits unilaterally.

2. **Allocation by State Capacity**
   - Each Gate Member's share of the annual quota is allocated proportionally to:
     - Demonstrated labour market demand (sectoral employment data)
     - Reception capacity (housing, social services, integration infrastructure)
     - Historical labour migration levels
   - Allocation is decided by the Gate Council annually by double majority (3 states, >126.5M population).

3. **Allocation Example (indicative)**
   - Germany: 6,000–7,000 permits
   - France: 4,000–5,000 permits
   - Italy: 3,000–4,000 permits
   - Poland: 1,000–1,500 permits
   - Norway: 500–1,000 permits
   - **Total: 15,000–20,000 per annum**

4. **Permit Duration and Rights**
   - Permits are issued for 2-year renewable cycles.
   - Permit holders have employment rights, social security access, and family reunification eligibility under receiving Member law.
   - Permits are not conditional on staying in a single employer; permit holders may seek employment within their Member's labour market.

---

### Part 2: Partnership Corridor Framework

1. **Joint Processing Centres in Morocco**
   - Gate Hestia Member States, in partnership with Morocco, establish joint processing and documentation centres in Moroccan territory (Casablanca, Tangier, Marrakech, and one interior location as mutually agreed).
   - These are **not detention facilities** and **not asylum processing sites**.
   - They are visa/work-permit processing centres only.

2. **Processing Standards**
   - Applicants present documentation: identity, employment contract (pre-arranged with receiving Member employer), health certificate.
   - Processing timeframe: 14 days from application submission to permit issuance.
   - No political vetting, no security interviewing beyond standard background checks.
   - Decision is automatic if applicant meets criteria (identity verified, contract valid, health screened).

3. **Moroccan Sovereignty**
   - Processing centres operate on Moroccan soil under joint governance:
     - Morocco appoints one director; Gate Members appoint one director (rotated annually).
     - Morocco retains full police and judicial authority.
     - Gate Members provide technical staff (visa officers, administrators).
   - Processing centre staff are subject to Moroccan law for misconduct; Gate Members indemnify Morocco against liability.

4. **Staffing and Logistics**
   - Gate Members jointly fund and staff processing centres (€2M annually for 4 centres).
   - Morocco provides office space, utilities, and local security.
   - Gate Members' visa/labour officers rotate (2-year assignments).

---

### Part 3: Reciprocal Cooperation

1. **Maritime Safety and SAR Coordination**
   - Morocco agrees to:
     - Maintain active maritime patrol in Moroccan territorial waters and EEZ.
     - Participate in Mediterranean SAR coordination under 003-A (Anchor Hestia).
     - Provide advance notice to Gate/Anchor coordination authorities if Morocco's SAR assets are engaged in rescue operations.
   - Gate Hestia Members reciprocally agree to:
     - Provide Morocco with maritime safety training and equipment support (€5M, 5-year programme).
     - Include Morocco in Mediterranean meteorological and maritime data sharing (real-time storm warning, maritime surveillance feeds).

2. **Transit Management and Non-Instrumentalisation**
   - Gate Hestia Members acknowledge Morocco's role as a transit country with limited inland capacity.
   - Morocco is not required to detain or intercept migrant vessels; this remains Morocco's sovereign choice.
   - Gate Hestia does not seek to instrumentalise Morocco's police or military for deterrence purposes.
   - If Morocco detects irregular vessels transiting to Gate Hestia, notification is voluntary and offered without expectation of enforced interception.

3. **Capacity-Building and Development**
   - Gate Hestia commits to multilateral development support (separate budget line):
     - €2M annually for 5 years: port infrastructure in Tangier and Casablanca.
     - €1M annually for 5 years: maritime safety training and equipment.
     - €0.5M annually for 5 years: labour law enforcement and workplace safety.
   - This is distinct from processing centre costs and is provided whether or not Morocco accepts any regulatory obligation.

---

### Part 4: Partnership Language and Sovereignty Respect

1. **No Instrumentalisation Clause**
   - Morocco is recognised as a sovereign state with equal standing in this partnership.
   - Gate Hestia members do not condition other assistance, trade, or diplomatic relations on Morocco's participation in legal migration or SAR initiatives.
   - Morocco may opt out of any element of this framework with 90 days' notice without penalty or consequence.

2. **Explicit Sovereignty Protections**
   - Morocco retains full authority over its internal labour law, wages, working conditions, and employment sectors.
   - Gate Hestia does not dictate Moroccan employment policy or working standards.
   - Processing centres operate under Moroccan law; Gate Members' staff are subject to local jurisdiction.

3. **Non-Intervention**
   - Gate Hestia rejects any framing that Morocco is "responsible for" irregular migration or "obligated" to prevent departures.
   - Morocco's obligations are limited to:
     - Operating processing centres jointly (factual, not legal obligation).
     - Notifying Gate authorities of SAR operations if practicable (without prejudice to Morocco's sovereignty).
     - Maintaining normal diplomatic and police cooperation (existing obligations).

4. **Public Statements and Framing**
   - All Gate Hestia public communication on this partnership shall use language emphasizing cooperation, labour partnership, and mutual benefit.
   - Rhetoric such as "burden-sharing," "fortress Europe," "deterrence," or "Morocco's responsibility" is prohibited in official statements.

---

### Part 5: Review and Adjustment

1. **Annual Review Clause**
   - The Gate Council shall review this instrument annually, beginning Year 2 of implementation.
   - Review covers:
     - Quota adequacy and sectoral demand
     - Processing centre efficiency
     - SAR coordination outcomes
     - Morocco's assessment of partnership fairness and sovereignty respect

2. **Three-Year Full Assessment**
   - After 3 years of implementation, Gate Council conducts a comprehensive review.
   - If Morocco indicates dissatisfaction or requests modification, negotiation must occur.
   - If quota is not being used (less than 60% uptake over 3 years), quota may be adjusted downward or suspended.

3. **Opt-Out Provisions**
   - Morocco may opt out entirely with 90 days' notice. Gate Hestia shall continue development support for 1 year beyond termination.
   - Individual Gate Members may adjust their quota allocation downward by up to 30% with 1 year's notice, but cannot suspend participation entirely.

## Implementation Timeline

- **Month 1:** Gate Council approves instrument by double majority. Morocco formally acknowledges partnership framing and accepts Observer Seat co-sponsorship status for this initiative.
- **Months 2–4:** Processing centre sites finalised; staff recruited and trained.
- **Month 5:** Processing centres operational; first permits issued.
- **Year 1:** 7,500–10,000 permits issued (ramp-up phase).
- **Year 2+:** Full 15,000–20,000 quota operational.

## Co-Sponsorship and Accord State Status

**Morocco** co-sponsors this proposal via its Observer Seat status granted in Round 1. Under Charter VI.10 (Accord State participation), Morocco may co-sponsor and shape instruments affecting its interests, even though it cannot independently propose Gate instruments.

**Norway** co-sponsors as a Gate Member fully committed to legal labour migration pathways as an alternative to irregular migration and emergency relocation pressure.

**Italy** proposes this as the natural complement to DEU's 001 relocation mechanism and ITA's 003-A SAR coordination. Legal pathways reduce downstream arrival pressure and allow Morocco to participate constructively in Mediterranean safety governance.

---

## Governance and Integration with Other Instruments

1. **Relationship to 001 (DEU Solidarity Regulation)**
   - This instrument is complementary to 001. By providing legal pathways, it reduces irregular migration to Italy, thereby reducing relocation trigger frequency.
   - Persons arriving under this legal pathway are not subject to 001 relocation; they are admitted directly by their receiving Member under labour law.

2. **Relationship to 003-A (ITA SAR Core)**
   - This instrument complements 003-A by formalising Morocco's participation in Mediterranean safety coordination.
   - Morocco's willingness to participate in SAR notification (Part 3.1) strengthens 003-A's effectiveness without imposing enforcement obligations.

3. **Relationship to 004 (POL Eastern Border Fund)**
   - This instrument is independent of 004 and does not affect eastern border policy.

4. **Consistency with Charter Non-Refoulement Standards**
   - All persons, whether admitted under legal pathways or arriving irregularly, retain full asylum rights.
   - Processing centres are not asylum exclusion mechanisms.
   - No person may be returned to Morocco without individual assessment of protection claims (Bench Hestia oversight).

## Final Notes

This proposal signals a shift in Gate Hestia migration governance from emergency relocation to structured legal pathways. It is designed with Morocco as a true partner, not a border enforcement zone. ITA, NOR, and MAR commit to this framing and see it as a sustainable foundation for Mediterranean labour migration and SAR cooperation.
