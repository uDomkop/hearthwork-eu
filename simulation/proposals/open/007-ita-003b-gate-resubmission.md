# Proposal 007: Post-Rescue Reception and Relocation Clarification Regulation

## Purpose

This Gate Hestia instrument clarifies that persons rescued at sea and disembarked in Italy pursuant to the Mediterranean SAR Coordination and Funding Regulation (003-A, Anchor Hestia) are eligible for automatic relocation under the Gate Hestia Solidarity and Border Management Regulation (001, DEU) without further classification delay.

Persons certified as rescued at sea (as documented by SAR coordination authorities under 003-A) shall not be processed or managed as isolated reception matters; they shall trigger relocation shares under 001 as a distinct, certifiable category.

This is not a new relocation mechanism. It is a clarification that 001's relocation trigger applies to this specific, high-volume arrival category.

---

## Legislative Framework

### Part 1: Reception and Processing Window

1. **Disembarkation and Certification**
   - All persons rescued at sea in the Mediterranean are disembarked under 003-A coordination in the port of safest landing (typically Italy).
   - Upon disembarkation, persons are certified by the Maritime Authority (rotating under 003-A) as having been rescued at sea. This certification is administrative and non-discretionary.

2. **Processing Window**
   - A 30-day disembarkation and initial processing window begins on the date of disembarkation.
   - During this window, Italy shall:
     - Conduct initial health and safety screening
     - Register persons for documentation purposes
     - Provide reception (food, shelter, basic healthcare)
   - All costs during the processing window are covered by the External Border and Reception Fund (001, Article 3).

3. **Automatic Relocation Activation**
   - At day 31 of disembarkation, if persons remain in Italy, automatic relocation shares under 001 Article 2.1 activate.
   - Italy shall notify the Gate Council of the cohort size and the automatic share calculation.
   - No separate application, proof of burden, or capacity review is required. Relocation shares activate by operation of law.

---

### Part 2: Cross-Reference with 001 (Solidarity Regulation)

1. **Binding Character**
   - The automatic relocation mechanism in 001 Article 2.1 is binding and mandatory.
   - Sea-rescue arrivals are not subject to opt-out, voluntary negotiation, or capacity-based exemption.
   - All Gate Hestia Members have equal obligations under 001 for this category.

2. **Relocation Share Calculation**
   - Shares are calculated by Member population (001 Article 2.1).
   - Italy's share burden is excluded from the divisor pool for its own disembarked cohort (standard relocation arithmetic — Italy cannot relocation to itself).

3. **Fund Coverage**
   - External Border and Reception Fund (001, Article 3) covers:
     - Italy's disembarkation and 30-day processing costs (€15,000 per person)
     - Relocation transfer logistics
     - Receiving Member reception costs (€15,000 per person for 1 year from relocation)

4. **No Instrumentalisation of 003-A**
   - This instrument does not impose additional SAR obligations on Anchor Members or alter 003-A funding.
   - 003-A remains a humanitarian rescue coordination framework.
   - This instrument simply clarifies the downstream legal status of rescue arrivals under 001.

---

### Part 3: Disembarkation and Reception Standards

1. **Non-Refoulement and Due Process**
   - No person certified as rescued at sea may be returned to sea before the 30-day processing window.
   - All persons retain full procedural rights for asylum and protection claims under Bench Hestia processes.
   - Return to third countries occurs only after individual determination by competent national authorities and Bench-reviewed due process.

2. **Reception Conditions**
   - Italy shall maintain reception conditions in compliance with Charter standards (Bench Hestia).
   - Crowding, unsanitary conditions, or inhumane treatment trigger Bench Hestia oversight and are grounds for Member challenge.

3. **Documentation**
   - Italy shall provide transparent, monthly reports to the Gate Council on:
     - Number of persons disembarked
     - Date of disembarkation
     - Relocation shares activated
     - Outcomes (relocation completed, asylum granted, returned, etc.)

---

## Implementation Timeline

- **Day 1 (disembarkation):** Certification by Maritime Authority; processing window begins; costs to External Border and Reception Fund.
- **Day 31:** Automatic relocation shares activate; Italy notifies Gate Council.
- **Day 32–60:** Receiving Members assume responsibility; Fund covers relocation transfer and reception.
- **Year 1:** Receiving Members provide reception under Fund protection.
- **Year 2+:** If persons remain, national responsibility applies (standard immigration law).

---

## Relationship to 001 (Amendment Notes)

This proposal is designed to work with 001 as enacted. If DEU amends 001 Article 2.3(d) (as proposed in DEU's amendment submission 005), this instrument's clarification becomes even stronger — the narrowed SAR reference in 001 will cleanly separate Anchor coordination from Gate relocation.

ITA and DEU co-sponsor this as the natural complement to 001. The relocation mechanism enacted in Round 1 was a landmark achievement; this clarification ensures it delivers relief to Italy by applying it to the largest, most identifiable arrival category.

---

## Co-Sponsorship Note

**DEU** co-sponsors this proposal. DEU's enactment of 001 created the legal architecture; ITA's gate-resubmission of 003-B ensures that architecture is applied to the crisis it was built to address.

This is not a DEU amendment. It is a separate, complementary Gate instrument that confirms 001's relocation mechanism covers sea arrivals.
