# Mediterranean Search and Rescue Coordination and Funding Regulation

## Preamble

The Mediterranean Sea remains one of the world's busiest maritime corridors and a critical route for international commerce, fisheries, and lawful maritime transit. Rising numbers of persons in distress at sea — whether migrants, asylum seekers, or others — require a coordinated maritime response grounded in international maritime law, humanitarian obligation, and professional rescue standards. The current fragmented approach, in which individual coastal states bear disproportionate operational and financial burdens, is unsustainable and fails to meet the professional standards expected of the Hearthwork framework. A unified maritime rescue coordination system, funded across Anchor Hestia members, ensures equitable burden-sharing among states with maritime interests and upholds the humanitarian principles that bind the Union.

## Article 1 — Establishment of the Mediterranean Maritime Rescue Coordination System

1. The Anchor Hestia hereby establishes a **Mediterranean Maritime Rescue Coordination System** (hereafter "the System"), effective immediately upon enactment.

2. The System shall be a coordinated maritime rescue regime governing search and rescue (SAR) operations in international and territorial waters of the Mediterranean Sea, including the areas under the jurisdiction of participant states bordering the Mediterranean.

3. The System shall operate under the framework of the International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea (SOLAS) and the International Convention on Maritime Search and Rescue (SAR Convention), as amended. Nothing in this Regulation shall diminish the legal obligations of any state under international maritime law.

4. Participation in the System is mandatory for all Anchor Hestia members with maritime responsibility in Mediterranean waters. Anchor Hestia members without Mediterranean coastlines shall contribute to the funding mechanism established in Article 2.

## Article 2 — Common Maritime Rescue Fund

1. A **Common Maritime Rescue Fund** (hereafter "the Fund") is established within the Anchor Hestia budget, effective the first day of the calendar quarter following enactment.

2. The Fund shall be financed by:
   - Mandatory annual contributions from all Anchor Hestia members, calculated proportionally to the size of each member's Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) in Mediterranean waters, or where no EEZ exists, a per-capita baseline contribution;
   - Voluntary supplementary contributions from any Anchor member or third party;
   - Recovered costs from commercial entities whose vessels or personnel are rescued.

3. Fund management shall be delegated to a **Maritime Rescue Directorate** (hereafter "the Directorate"), appointed by the Anchor Hestia Council and answerable to it quarterly.

4. The Directorate shall:
   - Allocate Fund resources to participant states for operational SAR activities (vessels, personnel, training, communication infrastructure);
   - Ensure standardized training and certification for SAR personnel across the System;
   - Maintain a unified maritime distress communication protocol;
   - Publish quarterly operational reports on rescues, assets deployed, and costs incurred.

## Article 3 — Coordination and Professional Standards

1. All maritime rescue operations within the System's area of responsibility shall be coordinated through a **Single Maritime Authority** (hereafter "the Authority"). The Authority shall be established by the Directorate and staffed jointly by personnel from participating coastal states.

2. The Authority shall:
   - Receive and process all maritime distress signals within the area;
   - Designate which participating state's assets shall respond to any given distress call, based on proximity, capability, and availability;
   - Ensure that no distress signal is unattended;
   - Maintain standardized protocols for transfer of rescued persons to safe ports, consistent with the SAR Convention.

3. All rescued persons shall be transferred to a place of safety in accordance with international maritime law. The determination of "place of safety" shall be guided by:
   - Proximity to the rescue location;
   - Availability of port facilities;
   - The wishes of the rescued persons where practicable;
   - The principle of equitable distribution of rescued persons among participating states' safe ports, subject to a quarterly review by the Directorate.

4. All participating states undertake to maintain port facilities capable of receiving and processing rescued persons within their waters, and to provide humanitarian assistance (medical, food, shelter) during processing pending further arrangements.

## Article 4 — Rotating Flagship Coordination

1. The Directorate shall establish a **Rotating Flagship Coordination** program. A lead coastal state shall be designated to command the unified maritime rescue coordination for a period of twelve calendar months. This role shall rotate among all Anchor Hestia members with Mediterranean coastlines, in alphabetical order by state code.

2. The Flagship Coordinator shall:
   - Exercise operational command of the Single Maritime Authority;
   - Deploy additional assets from its own fleet if required by the System;
   - Submit monthly operational summaries to the Directorate;
   - Serve as the primary liaison with international maritime organizations (IMO, IHO, regional bodies).

3. The cost of exercising the Flagship role, beyond routine personnel costs, shall be reimbursed by the Fund.

## Article 5 — Reporting and Transparency

1. The Directorate shall maintain a public registry of all SAR operations within the System's area, including:
   - Date, time, and location of each rescue;
   - Number of persons rescued and their reported circumstances;
   - Responding state and assets deployed;
   - Port of disembarkation;
   - Estimated operational cost.

2. The registry shall be published quarterly and made available to all Anchor Hestia members, the Assembly, and the public. Personal identifiers shall be redacted where required by national data protection law.

3. The Directorate shall issue an annual report to the Anchor Hestia Council on:
   - Total rescue operations and persons assisted;
   - Operational costs and Fund allocation;
   - Performance of the coordination system;
   - Any systemic gaps or recommendations for amendment.

## Article 6 — Vessel and Personnel Standards

1. All vessels designated for maritime rescue under the System shall meet minimum safety and operational standards established by the Directorate in consultation with maritime authorities of participant states.

2. All SAR personnel shall complete standardized training certified by the Directorate. Training curricula shall be harmonized across participating states and shall emphasize international humanitarian norms, maritime law, and inter-state coordination.

3. The cost of training and certification shall be borne by the Fund.

## Article 7 — Relationship to Other Hestias

1. This Regulation is classified as an Anchor Hestia instrument under the Hearthwork Charter (Article II, Hestia Descriptions). It governs maritime rescue coordination and humanitarian response at sea.

2. Nothing in this Regulation shall:
   - Establish or modify border controls or immigration procedures (those remain within the Gate Hestia's competence);
   - Create obligations regarding the status determination, asylum processing, or relocation of rescued persons (those are separate matters for other Hestias or the Assembly);
   - Limit the sovereign authority of any state to manage its own territorial waters or to determine the legal status of persons within its jurisdiction;
   - Prevent any state from seeking additional cooperation on migration, asylum, or border management through other frameworks.

## Article 8 — Implementation

1. This Regulation enters into force on the first day of the calendar quarter following enactment, or immediately if enacted by an extraordinary majority of the Anchor Hestia Council.

2. The Directorate shall be constituted within 60 days of enactment. The first Single Maritime Authority shall be operational within 120 days.

3. Participating coastal states undertake to designate vessels and personnel to the System within 90 days of enactment.

4. The Flagship Coordination program shall commence with the first annual rotation on the first day of the second calendar quarter following enactment.

5. Until the Fund is fully operational, interim costs of coordinated SAR operations shall be reimbursed by the Anchor Hestia Council from the general budget, with final settlement conducted quarterly.

## Article 9 — Amendment and Review

1. This Regulation may be amended by the Anchor Hestia Council by a simple majority vote, except that changes to the funding formula (Article 2) shall require a double majority.

2. The Directorate shall conduct a comprehensive review of the System's operation within 24 months of enactment and shall report findings and recommendations to the Anchor Hestia Council.

## Fiscal Implications

- **Budget Impact**: Estimated EUR 120–180 million annually for coordinated maritime rescue operations (vessels, personnel, fuel, communications, administration).
- **Fiscal Key**: Mandatory Anchor Hestia Fiscal Key contribution required from all Anchor members. Non-payment triggers the Light-Out Clause (Charter VI.4).
- **Cost Distribution**: Annual contributions to the Fund shall be calculated as follows:
  - 40% by proportional share of Mediterranean EEZ area (only Anchor members with Mediterranean coastlines);
  - 30% per-capita (all Anchor members);
  - 30% proportional to each member's share of Anchor Hestia participation in other instruments (baseline fairness adjustment).
- **Reimbursement of coastal states**: Operational costs incurred by designated SAR vessels shall be fully reimbursed from the Fund within 90 days of submission of receipted invoices and operational logs.

## Notes for ILCB

This proposal is classified as an **Anchor Hestia** instrument because:

1. **Primary subject matter is maritime rescue coordination**, not border management. The System operates under the SOLAS and SAR Conventions, which are maritime instruments, not migration or border instruments.

2. **Humanitarian rescue at sea is a core maritime function** of all maritime states, predating and transcending immigration policy. The Anchor Hestia encompasses maritime cooperation and professional nautical standards.

3. **The proposal explicitly carves out border-control and status-determination functions** (Article 7.2). It does not purport to regulate immigration, asylum, relocation, or free movement — those remain with other Hestias.

4. **All Anchor members have maritime interests**, including landlocked or non-Mediterranean states, because maritime safety and rescue is a shared professional and humanitarian concern across the Union.

5. **Cost-spreading across Anchor (not only Gate) reflects the maritime character**: while Gate members are concerned with migration management, Anchor members are concerned with maritime safety. This proposal addresses maritime safety.

The ILCB should rule on the text as written. ITA notes that careful framing of this proposal prioritizes maritime and humanitarian aspects to ensure fair classification. We respect the ILCB's independent judgment.
