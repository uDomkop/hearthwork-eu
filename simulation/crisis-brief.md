# Crisis Brief — The Adriatic Surge

**Session 1 — Gate Hestia Emergency Sitting**

---

## Situation

A prolonged civil conflict in the eastern Mediterranean basin has displaced approximately 1.4 million people over eighteen months. The conflict is ongoing; no near-term resolution is expected.

In the past ninety days:

- **340,000 arrivals** by sea, the majority landing in ITA (southern ports) and secondarily in GRC (not a session participant, but a Gate Hestia member in distress).
- **ITA** has formally invoked the Gate Hestia's emergency consultation clause, declaring its reception capacity exhausted.
- **FRA** has reimposed internal Schengen border controls on its Italian and Spanish borders, citing secondary movement pressure. This action has not been submitted to the Gate Hestia Council — it was announced unilaterally.
- **POL** reports a separate pressure: 80,000 people have crossed or attempted to cross its eastern border in the same period, routed through a third country. POL has deployed military personnel to support border guards.
- **NOR** has not closed borders but has tightened processing times and is experiencing political pressure domestically. As a Companion, NOR is bound by Schengen and cannot legally close borders without Gate Hestia authorisation.
- **DEU** has seen a significant increase in onward movements from ITA and is under domestic pressure to act, but has not taken unilateral border measures.
- **MAR** is a significant transit country. Its cooperation has materially reduced arrivals in previous years. MAR has signalled that expanded cooperation is available — at a price.

---

## The Legal Tension

FRA's unilateral border closure is the immediate procedural problem. Free movement is an Assembly-Locked Domain (I.10). A Gate Hestia rule that purports to authorise suspension of free movement would be ultra vires. However, the Assembly has never legislated a crisis procedure for Schengen integrity. The gap is real.

The ILCB has not yet been asked to classify any proposal. The session opens now.

## Known Hestia Overlaps (ILCB pre-note)

The following overlaps are flagged in advance. Any proposal touching these intersections will be examined closely:

| Domain | Overlap |
|---|---|
| **Gate + Anchor** | Search and rescue operations in international waters — maritime (Anchor) or border management (Gate)? |
| **Gate + Shield** | Military personnel at POL eastern border — border management (Gate) or defence (Shield)? |
| **Gate + Bench** | Asylum procedure harmonisation — judicial cooperation (Bench) or migration management (Gate)? |
| **Gate + Assembly-Locked** | Any measure that suspends, conditions, or alters free movement for Union citizens is Assembly-Locked (I.10) and cannot be legislated by the Gate Hestia alone |

## Opening Positions (known to all participants)

| State | Public position |
|---|---|
| **DEU** | Wants binding relocation mechanism; will fund it; will not accept indefinite status quo |
| **FRA** | Wants border hardening and external processing; will not accept mandatory quotas |
| **ITA** | Wants immediate relocation relief; threatens to cease cooperation with Gate Hestia rules unless burden-sharing is resolved |
| **POL** | Will accept external border fund; will not accept relocation quotas under any framing |
| **NOR** | Wants Schengen integrity preserved; prepared to contribute financially; watching FRA's border closure closely |
| **MAR** | Wants expanded legal labour migration pathways and development funding; will cooperate on transit in exchange |
