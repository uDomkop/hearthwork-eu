**Outcome:** ENACTED — 4-1, 169M-84M. Passes over DEU's objection. DEU's NO held firm on the red line that even 14 days of permissible unilateral border action crosses a constitutional threshold. The instrument passes because FRA+ITA+POL+NOR form a 169M coalition well above the 126.5M threshold.

**NOR Suspensive Veto notice (VII.5): formally withdrawn.** NOR's conditions — self-executing sunset, Companion deliberative inclusion — were met by FRA's ILCB compliance amendments. The constitutional standoff over FRA's border closure is resolved procedurally through 006's notification pathway.

**DEU's dissent is noted.** The 14-day sunset window represents a real tension: the instrument permits unilateral action to exist legally for a fixed period. DEU views this as a violation; the 4-state majority views the self-executing expiry as an adequate constitutional safeguard. The ILCB's Ruling H cleared the instrument; the political disagreement stands.
