# Hearthwork EU Charter

---

## **Preamble**

The peoples and states of Europe,

Recognizing the achievements of a Union founded on law, rights, and shared purpose;

Acknowledging the necessity for unity to evolve into a plurality of participation without compromising the coherence of our order;

Affirming that democratic voice is intrinsically linked to shared obligation;

Determined to establish a Union that is **flexible, accountable, and responsive to its citizens**;

Hereby adopt this Charter as the constitutional foundation of a European Union composed of **Full Members, Inner Union Associates, Outer Union Associates, and Strategic Partners**.

---

# **Title I — Foundational Principles**

### **I.1 — Equal Sovereignty**

All states retain their sovereignty. Differences in standing reflect voluntary levels of integration, not hierarchy.

### **I.2 — Standings of Participation**

The Union recognizes five distinct standings:

1. **Full Members**: Participate in all Hestias, with the option to opt out of specific domains.
2. **Inner Union Associates**: Engage in a broad range of Hestias, with a defined pathway to Full Membership.
3. **Outer Union Associates**: Participate in a limited set of Hestias, retaining flexibility to deepen integration over time.
4. **Strategic Partners**: Non-EU states with consultative rights in Hestias addressing global challenges, such as trade or climate policy.

**Note:** The protective framework of the Union extends to all entities that uphold its values and principles, regardless of their formal standing.

---

# **Title II — Hestias and Governance**

### **II.1 — Hierarchy of Rules**

1. The Assembly Baseline establishes the minimum standards applicable to all members.
2. Hestias may adopt more stringent rules but cannot derogate from the Baseline unless explicitly authorized by a two-thirds majority of the Assembly.
3. In the event of conflict, the Baseline prevails unless the Hestia explicitly derogates from it.

### **II.2 — Hestia Framework**

Each Hestia shall include:

- Clearly defined goals and scope.
- A transparent funding mechanism, such as GDP-based contributions.
- A structured decision-making process, e.g., qualified majority voting.
- Rules governing entry and exit.

---

# **Title III — Citizen Participation and Transparency**

### **III.1 — Citizen Engagement**

1. Each Hestia shall establish a Citizen Panel composed of 100 residents from participating states.
  - Panel members shall be selected through a **transparent and stratified random process**, ensuring representation across demographics, regions, and socioeconomic backgrounds.
2. The Panel shall:
  - Review and provide feedback on proposals prior to voting.
  - Publish public reports on the potential impacts of proposed measures.

### **III.2 — Transparency Tools**

The Assembly shall develop and maintain a **"My EU" portal**, enabling citizens to:

- Monitor their state’s participation in Hestias.
- Simulate the potential impacts of joining or leaving specific Hestias.
- Submit questions and petitions to their representatives.

---

# **Title IV — Decision-Making and Power Balance**

### **IV.1 — Decision-Making in Hestias**

Decisions within Hestias require:

- A majority of participating states.
- A majority of the participating population (or GDP, where relevant).

### **IV.2 — Hestia Leadership**

The presidency of each Hestia shall rotate annually among participating states, with priority given to smaller states to ensure equitable representation.

---

# **Title V — Crisis and Emergency Measures**

### **V.1 — Emergency Provisions**

1. A predefined set of emergency measures (e.g., joint procurement, border controls) may be activated by a simple majority of the Assembly.
2. Emergency measures shall expire after 90 days unless explicitly ratified for extension.
3. A public debriefing shall be conducted within 30 days of the measure’s expiration to ensure accountability and transparency.

---

# **Title VI — Independent Oversight**

### **VI.1 — Independent Bodies**

- Nominations for Triple Key bodies shall be subject to public hearings, including live Q&A sessions with candidates.
- Members shall serve a single six-year term, followed by a three-year cooling-off period.

---

# **Title VII — Pilot Implementation and Review**

### **VII.1 — Pilot Phase**

1. The Assembly shall launch a pilot Hestia within 12 months of this Charter’s adoption.
2. The pilot shall evaluate:
  - The effectiveness of opt-in/opt-out mechanisms.
  - The functionality of citizen participation tools.
  - The robustness of conflict resolution processes.

### **VII.2 — Annual Review**

The Assembly shall conduct an annual review to simplify and improve one aspect of the Charter, based on feedback from citizens and member states.

---

This Charter is a living document, designed to evolve in accordance with the needs and aspirations of the peoples of Europe.
